Heart (ECG) BioAmp Arduino Firmware#

What is Electrocardiography (ECG)?#

Electrocardiography [1] is the process of producing an electrocardiogram (ECG or EKG), a recording of the heart‚Äôs electrical activity through repeated cardiac cycles. It is an electrogram of the heart which is a graph of voltage versus time of the electrical activity of the heart using electrodes placed on the skin. These electrodes detect the small electrical changes that are a consequence of cardiac muscle depolarization followed by repolarization during each cardiac cycle (heartbeat).

2. ECG Filter

1. Program Purpose & Overview

The ECG Filter program passes your raw ECG through a digital band‚Äëpass filter (0.5‚Äì44.5‚ÄØHz), removing baseline drift and high‚Äëfrequency noise so you see only the heart‚Äôs true waveform.

Real ECGs have very slow wandering trends (e.g. breathing‚Äërelated drift) and high‚Äëfrequency interference (muscle noise, powerline hum). A band‚Äëpass filter isolates the clinically relevant 0.5‚Äì44‚ÄØHz band where the QRS complex and T‚Äëwaves live.

2. How It Works

  • Uses a bandpass IIR filter order and coefficients chosen to sharply cut below 0.5‚ÄØHz and above 44.5‚ÄØHz.

  • Applies filter sample‚Äëby‚Äësample inside the same fixed‚Äërate loop.

  • Prints filtered values to Serial Monitor.

  • To learn more about filters and how to generate new filters, visit: https://docs.scipy.org/doc/scipy/reference/generated/scipy.signal.butter.html

3. Heart Rate Detection

The Heart Rate Detection transforms your filtered ECG signal into a live, numerical heart‚Äërate readout. As the Arduino continuously samples the cleaned‚Äëup ECG waveform from the BioAmp EXG Pill, this sketch identifies each heartbeat‚Äôs characteristic R‚Äëwave peak and timestamps its occurrence. By measuring the time between successive peaks, it calculates your beats per minute (BPM) and prints the result to the Serial Monitor, giving you a real‚Äëtime view of your current heart rate without any external software or display.

Under the hood, the sketch operates like a simple digital stethoscope: it maintains a running scan of the incoming ECG, and whenever the signal sharply rises above a chosen threshold‚Äîindicating an R‚Äëwave‚Äîit marks that as one beat. It then computes the interval since the previous beat and applies the standard conversion (60‚ÄØ000‚ÄØms divided by the interval) to determine BPM. This approach delivers an immediate and intuitive heart‚Äërate measurement, turning raw biopotential spikes into meaningful, human‚Äëreadable data.

For detailed guide, visit our Instructables Page: Measuring Heart Rate Using BioAmp EXG Pill

4. Heart Beat Detection

The Heart Beat Detection program converts your filtered ECG waveform into a reliable count of heartbeats by monitoring the signal‚Äôs ‚Äúenergy‚Äù rather than raw peaks. As the Arduino continuously reads the cleaned ECG from the BioAmp EXG Pill, this sketch computes a short‚Äëterm measure of signal variability‚Äîessentially a running envelope or standard deviation of the voltage. Whenever that envelope surges above a tuned threshold (indicating the rapid voltage change of an R‚Äëwave), the code registers a single heartbeat and then waits for the signal to subside below the threshold again, ensuring each beat is counted only once.

By tracking the moments when the envelope crosses its threshold, the sketch builds a real‚Äëtime tally of your heartbeats. Rather than simply looking for voltage spikes, it uses the envelope to smooth out noise and baseline drift, making it far more robust in everyday, less‚Äëcontrolled settings. The result is a dependable heartbeat counter that works even if the ECG amplitude varies or your electrodes shift slightly‚Äîperfect for basic heart‚Äërate monitoring without complex peak‚Äëdetection circuitry.

6. Faster Heart Rate Detection

1. Program Purpose & Overview

The Faster Heart Rate Detection sketch delivers the same beat‚Äëto‚ÄëBPM functionality as its predecessor but in a highly optimized package. It reads your ECG from the BioAmp EXG Pill and Muscle BioAmp Shield at a controlled 125‚ÄØHz rate, applies a four‚Äëstage Butterworth band‚Äëpass IIR filter to isolate the 0.5‚Äì44.5‚ÄØHz heartband, then detects each R‚Äëwave using a running‚Äëbuffer envelope method. Rather than simple threshold‚Äëcrossing, it uses a small circular window of recent samples to compute both the mean and standard deviation, flagging a heartbeat only when the incoming sample exceeds the current mean by more than half the window‚Äôs standard deviation and respects a brief debounce interval.

2. How It Works

  • Fixed‚Äêrate sampling at 125‚ÄØHz using a microsecond timer (micros() + timer loop) ensures consistent data intervals with very little timing overhead.

  • Inlined 4‚Äësection Butterworth IIR filter (ECGFilter()) removes baseline drift and high‚Äëfrequency noise in a single pass, avoiding function‚Äëcall costs.

  • Envelope‚Äëbased peak detection over a 26‚Äësample circular buffer computes mean and standard deviation on the fly, flagging a heartbeat only when the current sample exceeds mean + 0.5 √ó stddev.

  • Debounce logic (peakCooldownPeriod = 2‚ÄØms) uses just two Booleans and a timestamp to prevent false re‚Äëtriggers on the same beat.

  • Circular timing buffers (timeBuffer/sumBuffer, size‚ÄØ5) average recent inter‚Äëbeat intervals before converting to BPM, smoothing out irregularities without large loops.
